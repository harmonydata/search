Recent corporate scandals (collapse of Lehmans, ‘Hackgate’, Barclays/LIBOR, Standard Chartered Bank/Iran etc) bring into sharp focus the power of non-state actors and their capacity to impact on society. Such scandals also raise questions over the extent to which lawyers are or have been involved in corporate wrongdoing and how or if those lawyers can shape and control corporate actors.
