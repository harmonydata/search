    • British Autism Study of Infant Siblings (BASIS)

    The British Autism Study of Infant Siblings (BASIS) is a significant longitudinal research project conducted in the UK, focusing on infants who are at an elevated likelihood (EL) for autism. The primary aim of BASIS is to identify early markers and developmental trajectories associated with autism by studying infants who have older siblings diagnosed with autism, as these infants are at a higher risk of developing the condition themselves. The study employs innovative techniques to assess both brain and behavioral development in these infants, allowing researchers to observe potential differences in developmental patterns compared to infants without a familial history of autism. BASIS has expanded its scope to also include infants at elevated likelihood for Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD), recognizing the overlapping and distinct developmental pathways of these neurodevelopmental disorders. By following these infants over time, BASIS aims to improve the understanding of early developmental processes and contribute to the early identification and intervention strategies for autism and ADHD. The findings from BASIS have significant implications for early diagnosis and the development of targeted interventions, potentially improving outcomes for children at risk for these conditions.
